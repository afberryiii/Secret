The Nightmare's End

You plaster on a genuine smile, pushing the lingering dread of the nightmare to the back of your mind. "Yeah, it was a great night," you say, your voice steadier now. "I'm just glad it's the weekend. I can't wait to just chill and not think about college applications for five minutes." A collective sigh of relief goes through the group, and the conversation shifts seamlessly to the mundane details of their summer plans. Kevin talks about his baseball team's upcoming tournament, Sarah complains about her part-time job at the local diner, and David excitedly details his plans for a new computer build. You find yourself laughing along with them, the familiar rhythm of your friendships a comforting blanket against the cold unease that had been nipping at your heels. You glance at Chloe. She's talking with Maya about a new art exhibit, her face animated and carefree. Seeing her so happy, so safe, you can't help but feel a wave of relief. It was just a dream. A bad one, sure, but nothing more. The bell rings, and the group disperses. You and Chloe walk to your first class together, the conversation light and easy. The morning sun streams through the school's windows, making the air feel warm and bright. You're safe. You're home. The nightmare is over. But as you reach your classroom, a single text message notification vibrates on your phone. You pull it out, a knot of dread tightening in your stomach. The message is from an unsaved number, and it reads: "Nice try. See you in class." Your blood runs cold. The phone slips from your grasp, clattering to the floor. The voice from your nightmare was a chillingly familiar echo, but the message is an even colder, more terrifying reality. This isn't a dream. This is real.
