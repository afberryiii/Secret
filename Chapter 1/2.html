The Nightmare's End

You jolt awake with a gasp, your body drenched in a cold sweat. The digital numbers on your alarm clock glow menacingly: 6:00 AM. The sound of the alarm is a jarring intrusion, pulling you from the terrifying dream. The events of the night before—the prom, the whispered threats, Ghostface, the chase—all of it dissolves into the hazy unreality of a nightmare. A text message notification on your phone reminds you that it's Monday morning, the first day of the rest of your life. The message is from Chloe: "Hey, are you up? The prom was so much fun! See you at school." You look at your phone, the screen a stark contrast to the fear that still clings to your mind. The dream was so real. Too real. You shake your head, trying to clear the lingering terror, as the scent of bacon from downstairs reminds you that reality—and school—awaits.
